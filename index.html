Frequently Asked Questions

What is National Voter Registration Day?

National Voter Registration Day is a national holiday celebrating our democracy. It is a nonpartisan event held on the fourth Tuesday of September and has been widely celebrated by election officials, academic institutions, corporations, media partners, and nonprofits in the past. The holiday is supported by the National Association of Secretaries of State. It is further supported by the National Association of State Election Directors.

What does National Voter Registration Day accomplish?

In 2016 over 750,000 voters used National Voter Registration Day to register to vote across all 50 states.

Each year the holiday’s growing number of local partners engage upwards to 10,000 local volunteers.

Millions of voters need to register and re-register every year. By utilizing new technology and leveraging partners, we’ll educate Americans in all 50 states about how to register, sign up for election reminders, check their registration online, get mail ballots, learn about early voting and more.

National Voter Registration Day is a day of civic unity. It’s an opportunity to set aside differences and celebrate democracy and the rights and opportunities we all share as Americans.

Why is Harvard participating?

Harvard is a leading institution in so many ways; but we fall short when it comes to civic participation.

  • In 2014, only 24% of enrolled, eligible students at Harvard University voted.
  • In 2012, only 49% of enrolled, eligible students at Harvard University voted.

We want to create a culture of civic participation at Harvard and make sure every student has access to the information and tools they need to vote.

Why are we celebrating in 2017? It’s not an election year.

It IS an election year!

We want to make sure everyone is aware that almost every state has incredibly important local elections on November 7, 2017 – including Massachusetts!

What can I do at my school to participate?

We have given you a few examples of ways we’re celebrating here at Harvard College and Harvard Kennedy School: pizza parties, tables with voter registration forms, big events.

You can start smaller –a simple reminder on September 26 that it is National Voter Registration Day to the faculty and students in your school can go a long way!
